    Glossary¶

    100+ terms following ISO 11179 standards — precise, concise, non-circular definitions.

    A¶

    Adiabatic quantum computing A model of quantum computation in which a quantum system is initialized in the ground state of a simple Hamiltonian and evolved slowly to the ground state of a problem Hamiltonian, encoding the solution to an optimization problem.

    Amplitude A complex number α associated with a quantum state |ψ⟩ in a superposition; the probability of observing that state upon measurement equals |α|².

    Amplitude damping channel A quantum noise channel modeling energy relaxation (T1 decay), in which the |1⟩ state decays to |0⟩ with probability γ = 1 − e^{−t/T1}.

    Ancilla qubit A qubit used as temporary scratch space in a quantum circuit, typically initialized to |0⟩ and uncomputed after use to avoid unwanted entanglement.

    B¶

    BB84 protocol A quantum key distribution protocol (Bennett & Brassard, 1984) in which Alice encodes bits as qubits in randomly chosen bases and Bob measures in randomly chosen bases; security derives from the no-cloning theorem and measurement disturbance.

    Bell state Any of the four maximally entangled two-qubit states: |Φ±⟩ = (|00⟩±|11⟩)/√2 and |Ψ±⟩ = (|01⟩±|10⟩)/√2.

    Bell's theorem A theorem (Bell, 1964) showing that no local hidden variable theory can reproduce all predictions of quantum mechanics; the CHSH inequality provides a testable bound (S ≤ 2) violated by quantum mechanics (S = 2√2).

    Bivariate bicycle (BB) code A family of quantum low-density parity-check (qLDPC) codes, including the [[144,12,12]] gross code, that encodes 12 logical qubits into 144 data qubits — approximately 10× more efficient than the surface code.

    Bloch sphere A unit sphere whose surface represents all pure single-qubit states; north pole = |0⟩, south pole = |1⟩, equator = equal superpositions; interior points represent mixed states.

    BQP Bounded-error Quantum Polynomial time; the class of decision problems solvable by a quantum computer in polynomial time with error probability ≤ 1/3. Contains factoring (Shor's algorithm); believed not to contain NP-complete problems.

    C¶

    c-coupler An intra-chip long-range qubit connector demonstrated by IBM in 2025, enabling non-nearest-neighbor qubit interactions required by qLDPC codes on a 2D chip topology.

    CBOM (Cryptographic Bill of Materials) A structured inventory of all cryptographic algorithms, key sizes, libraries, and protocols in use across an organization's systems; the foundation for PQC migration planning.

    CHSH inequality An inequality (Clauser-Horne-Shimony-Holt, 1969) stating S ≤ 2 for any local hidden variable theory, where S is a function of four correlation measurements; quantum mechanics predicts S = 2√2.

    Circuit depth The number of sequential gate layers in a quantum circuit; limits computation to T2/t_gate gate operations before decoherence corrupts the result.

    Clifford group The group of quantum operations generated by H, S, and CNOT gates; efficient classical simulation is possible via the stabilizer formalism (Gottesman-Knill theorem).

    CNOT gate Controlled-NOT; a two-qubit gate that flips the target qubit if and only if the control qubit is |1⟩; the fundamental entangling gate in quantum circuits.

    Coherence time The time over which a qubit maintains its quantum properties; characterized by T1 (energy relaxation) and T2 (dephasing).

    Crypto-agility An architectural design principle enabling the replacement of cryptographic algorithms without redesigning the systems that use them; essential for PQC migration.

    D¶

    Decoherence The process by which a qubit becomes entangled with its environment, causing quantum phase information (off-diagonal density matrix elements) to leak into environmental degrees of freedom and become inaccessible.

    Density matrix A positive semi-definite Hermitian matrix ρ with Tr(ρ) = 1 representing the state of a quantum system; encompasses both pure states (Tr(ρ²) = 1) and mixed states (Tr(ρ²) < 1).

    Depolarizing channel A quantum noise channel that replaces a qubit state with the maximally mixed state I/2 with probability p; equivalent to applying X, Y, or Z each with probability p/4.

    Diffusion operator The operator D = 2|s⟩⟨s| − I used in Grover's algorithm to reflect amplitudes about their mean, amplifying the target state's amplitude.

    E¶

    Entanglement A property of a multi-qubit quantum state that cannot be written as a tensor product of individual qubit states; produces measurement correlations that violate Bell inequalities and are inexplicable by any local classical model.

    Entanglement entropy The von Neumann entropy S(ρA) of the reduced density matrix of one subsystem, quantifying the degree of entanglement in a bipartite pure state; ranges from 0 (product state) to 1 ebit (maximally entangled).

    F¶

    Fault-tolerant quantum computing Quantum computation in which errors are detected and corrected faster than they accumulate, enabling arbitrarily long computations; requires physical error rates below the code threshold.

    FIPS 203 Federal Information Processing Standard 203; the NIST standard for ML-KEM (Module-Lattice Key-Encapsulation Mechanism), the primary post-quantum key exchange algorithm, finalized August 2024.

    FIPS 204 NIST standard for ML-DSA (Module-Lattice Digital Signature Algorithm), the primary post-quantum signature algorithm, finalized August 2024.

    FIPS 205 NIST standard for SLH-DSA (Stateless Hash-Based Digital Signature Algorithm), based on SPHINCS+; provides algorithm diversity via hash-based security assumptions, finalized August 2024.

    G¶

    Gate fidelity A measure of how accurately a quantum gate is implemented; the overlap between the actual operation and the ideal unitary, typically expressed as 1 − error rate. Two-qubit gate fidelities of 99.5–99.99% are required for below-threshold operation.

    Gottesman-Knill theorem The theorem stating that quantum circuits composed only of Clifford gates (H, S, CNOT) can be efficiently simulated by a classical computer in O(n²) time, despite creating entanglement.

    Gross code The [[144,12,12]] bivariate bicycle code; IBM's primary qLDPC code for fault-tolerant computation, encoding 12 logical qubits into 144 data qubits at distance 12.

    Grover's algorithm A quantum algorithm (Grover, 1996) that searches an unsorted database of N items in O(√N) steps — a quadratic speedup over classical O(N) search; provably optimal for unstructured search (BBBV theorem).

    H¶

    Hadamard gate A single-qubit gate H = (1/√2)[[1,1],[1,−1]] that creates equal superposition from basis states: H|0⟩ = |+⟩, H|1⟩ = |−⟩. Self-inverse: H² = I.

    Harvest-now-decrypt-later (HNDL) attack A cryptographic attack strategy in which an adversary records currently encrypted communications for decryption once a cryptographically relevant quantum computer becomes available.

    Hilbert space A complete inner product space; the mathematical setting for quantum mechanics. An n-qubit system has a 2ⁿ-dimensional complex Hilbert space.

    I¶

    Ion trap A device using oscillating electric fields (Paul trap) or combined static and oscillating fields (Penning trap) to confine charged ions for use as qubits; used by IonQ and Quantinuum.

    K¶

    Kraus operators A set of matrices {K_k} satisfying ΣK_k†K_k = I that represent a quantum channel: ε(ρ) = ΣK_k ρ K_k†. Any physical quantum operation can be written in Kraus form.

    L¶

    l-coupler An inter-chip microwave entangling link planned by IBM for the Cockatoo processor (2027), enabling entanglement between separate quantum chip modules.

    Landauer's principle The principle that erasing one bit of information requires dissipating at least k_B T ln 2 joules of energy as heat, establishing a thermodynamic minimum cost for irreversible computation.

    Lattice A regular array of points in high-dimensional space L(B) = {Bz : z ∈ ℤⁿ}; the basis of post-quantum cryptography (LWE, ML-KEM, ML-DSA).

    Learning With Errors (LWE) A computational problem (Regev, 2005) of recovering a secret vector s from noisy linear equations As + e ≈ b (mod q); provably as hard as worst-case lattice problems and believed resistant to quantum attack.

    Logical qubit A qubit encoded into multiple physical qubits through quantum error correction, providing much lower error rates than physical qubits; the relevant unit of quantum computation in fault-tolerant systems.

    M¶

    Magic state distillation A fault-tolerant subroutine that produces high-fidelity non-Clifford ancilla states (magic states) from many low-fidelity copies; required for implementing T gates fault-tolerantly.

    Majorana zero mode A topological excitation in certain condensed matter systems (topological superconductors) proposed as the basis for topological qubits; claimed by Microsoft (Majorana 1, 2025) but disputed by the research community.

    ML-DSA Module-Lattice Digital Signature Algorithm; the NIST FIPS 204 post-quantum signature standard based on Module-LWE/SIS.

    ML-KEM Module-Lattice Key-Encapsulation Mechanism; the NIST FIPS 203 post-quantum key exchange standard based on Module-LWE.

    Mosca's theorem A risk planning framework: if data shelf-life + quantum migration time > time to CRQC, the data is at risk now and PQC migration should begin immediately.

    N¶

    NISQ (Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum) A descriptor (Preskill, 2018) for current quantum hardware with 50–1,000 physical qubits and no error correction; capable of quantum utility for narrow problems but not fault-tolerant quantum computation.

    No-cloning theorem The theorem (Wootters & Zurek, Dieks, 1982) that no unitary operation can copy an arbitrary unknown quantum state; follows from the linearity of quantum mechanics.

    P¶

    Pauli gates The three fundamental single-qubit operators X (bit-flip), Y (combined flip), Z (phase-flip); together with I, they form a basis for all single-qubit Hermitian operators.

    Phase-flip channel A quantum noise channel that applies Z with probability p, destroying off-diagonal coherences while preserving populations.

    Physical qubit An actual quantum two-level system (transmon, ion, atom, etc.) implementing one qubit in hardware; distinguished from the logical qubit it may help encode.

    Post-quantum cryptography (PQC) Cryptographic algorithms based on mathematical problems believed to be hard for both classical and quantum computers; NIST standardized ML-KEM, ML-DSA, and SLH-DSA in August 2024.

    Q¶

    QAOA (Quantum Approximate Optimization Algorithm) A hybrid classical-quantum algorithm using parameterized quantum circuits with alternating problem and mixer unitaries to approximate solutions to combinatorial optimization problems.

    QFT (Quantum Fourier Transform) A quantum analogue of the discrete Fourier transform that maps |j⟩ to (1/√N)Σe^{2πijk/N}|k⟩; implementable in O(n²) gates vs. classical FFT's O(N log N); the core of Shor's algorithm.

    qLDPC (Quantum Low-Density Parity-Check) code A family of quantum error correcting codes with constant encoding rate (number of logical qubits grows proportionally with physical qubits); includes IBM's bivariate bicycle codes.

    QUBO (Quadratic Unconstrained Binary Optimization) An optimization problem form: minimize xᵀQx for binary variables x; directly solvable by quantum annealers and equivalent to finding the ground state of an Ising Hamiltonian.

    Quantum advantage Demonstrably outperforming the best known classical algorithm on a commercially relevant problem using a quantum computer.

    Quantum annealing A non-ideal implementation of adiabatic quantum computing that exploits quantum tunneling to find low-energy states of an Ising Hamiltonian; used by D-Wave for combinatorial optimization.

    Quantum channel The most general physical operation on a quantum state; maps density matrices to density matrices while preserving positivity and trace, representable via Kraus operators.

    Quantum error correction (QEC) A set of techniques for detecting and correcting errors in quantum computations without measuring (and thus collapsing) the encoded quantum information.

    Quantum parallelism The simultaneous evolution of all 2ⁿ amplitude components of an n-qubit superposition under a single unitary operation; enables evaluating f(x) for all inputs simultaneously, though measurement yields only one outcome.

    Quantum supremacy Demonstrating that a quantum computer can perform any specific computation faster than any classical computer, regardless of practical utility (Google Sycamore 2019, Google Willow 2024).

    Quantum teleportation A protocol (Bennett et al., 1993) that transmits an unknown qubit state from Alice to Bob using one Bell pair and two classical bits, destroying the original state at Alice's location.

    Qubit The quantum mechanical analogue of a classical bit; a two-level quantum system described by the state |ψ⟩ = α|0⟩ + β|1⟩ where α,β ∈ ℂ and |α|² + |β|² = 1.

    R¶

    RSA A public-key cryptosystem (Rivest-Shamir-Adleman, 1977) whose security relies on the computational difficulty of factoring large integers; vulnerable to Shor's algorithm on a sufficiently large fault-tolerant quantum computer.

    S¶

    Shor's algorithm A quantum algorithm (Shor, 1994) that factors an integer N in O((log N)³) time — an exponential speedup over classical algorithms — by reducing factoring to period-finding solvable via the Quantum Fourier Transform.

    SLH-DSA Stateless Hash-Based Digital Signature Algorithm; NIST FIPS 205 post-quantum signature standard based on SPHINCS+; relies only on hash function security, providing algorithm diversity.

    Stabilizer code A quantum error correcting code defined by a set of commuting Pauli operators (stabilizers) whose +1 eigenspace is the code space; includes the surface code and bivariate bicycle codes.

    Stabilizer formalism A framework (Gottesman, 1997) for representing quantum states as the simultaneous +1 eigenstates of a set of commuting Pauli operators; enables efficient classical simulation of Clifford circuits and characterization of QEC codes.

    Superposition A quantum state that is a linear combination of multiple basis states: |ψ⟩ = Σαᵢ|i⟩; a consequence of the linearity of the Schrödinger equation. Not "both 0 and 1 simultaneously" but a continuous quantum state that collapses probabilistically upon measurement.

    Surface code A quantum error correcting code arranged on a 2D lattice of data and ancilla qubits, with stabilizers measuring the parity of neighboring qubits; the leading practical QEC code with a ~1% error threshold.

    T¶

    T gate A single-qubit phase gate T = diag(1, e^{iπ/4}); the non-Clifford gate that, when added to the Clifford set {H, S, CNOT}, makes the gate set universal for quantum computation.

    T1 Energy relaxation time; the characteristic time for a qubit to decay from |1⟩ to |0⟩ through energy exchange with the environment.

    T2 Dephasing time; the characteristic time for the relative phase between |0⟩ and |1⟩ to be randomized; always T2 ≤ 2T1.

    Threshold theorem The theorem (Aharonov-Ben-Or, Kitaev, Knill-Laflamme-Zurek, 1997) that if the physical error rate per gate is below a critical threshold p_th, fault-tolerant quantum computation of arbitrary length is possible; p_th ≈ 1% for the surface code.

    Toffoli gate A three-qubit gate (CCNOT) that flips the target qubit if and only if both control qubits are |1⟩; universal for classical reversible computation; bridges classical and quantum logic.

    Topological qubit A qubit whose quantum information is stored in global topological properties of a physical system rather than in local degrees of freedom, conferring inherent protection against local errors; proposed using Majorana zero modes (Microsoft).

    Transmon A type of superconducting qubit (a modified Cooper pair box) with weak anharmonicity, widely used by IBM and Google; typical T1 and T2 of 100–300 μs.

    U¶

    Unitary operator A linear operator U satisfying U†U = UU† = I; represents all valid quantum gate operations, preserves the norm of quantum states, and is always reversible (U⁻¹ = U†).

    V¶

    VQE (Variational Quantum Eigensolver) A hybrid classical-quantum algorithm that finds the ground state energy of a quantum system by minimizing the energy expectation value ⟨ψ(θ)|H|ψ(θ)⟩ over parameterized quantum circuits.

    Von Neumann entropy S(ρ) = −Tr(ρ log₂ ρ); the quantum analogue of Shannon entropy, quantifying the mixedness of a quantum state; equals 0 for pure states and n for the maximally mixed n-qubit state.

    Z¶

    Z gate The Pauli-Z gate, a single-qubit gate Z = diag(1,−1) that leaves |0⟩ unchanged and maps |1⟩ → −|1⟩; implements a phase flip without changing measurement probabilities in the computational basis.
