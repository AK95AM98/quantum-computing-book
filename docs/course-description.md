# Course Description

## Quantum Computing: From Qubits to Business Value — A Technical and Strategic Guide

**Author/Instructor:** Prof. Sharat Batra, University of Minnesota

---

## Audience

This book serves a **dual audience** simultaneously:

1. **CXOs and technology strategists** at enterprises evaluating quantum technology investments — including Chief Information Security Officers managing post-quantum cryptography migration, Chief Technology Officers assessing quantum computing timelines, and technology consultants advising on quantum readiness.

2. **Graduate students** in computer science, electrical engineering, physics, or MBA programs who need both rigorous technical foundations and strategic context.

**Audience level:** Graduate / professional development

---

## Prerequisites

- Linear algebra (vectors, matrices, eigenvalues, tensor products)
- Probability and statistics (expectation values, distributions)
- Basic classical computing concepts (bits, logic gates, algorithms, complexity classes P and NP)
- No prior quantum mechanics required

---

## Learning Outcomes

By completing this book, readers will be able to:

**Remember**
: Define qubit, superposition, entanglement, decoherence, logical qubit, quantum advantage, HNDL attack, ML-KEM, surface code, and threshold theorem.

**Understand**
: Explain why Shor's algorithm breaks RSA exponentially; how the threshold theorem enables fault-tolerant quantum computing; why lattice problems resist quantum speedup; and how modular chip architectures overcome single-die scaling limits.

**Apply**
: Calculate qubit overhead for surface codes and qLDPC codes; apply Mosca's theorem to enterprise data shelf-life; evaluate vendor roadmap claims against the five milestone criteria; implement Grover's algorithm in Qiskit.

**Analyze**
: Compare hardware modalities (superconducting, ion-trap, neutral atom, topological, photonic) on fidelity, coherence, scalability, and commercial maturity; distinguish delivered vs. projected vs. controversial milestones.

**Evaluate**
: Assess organizational quantum readiness using IBM QRI / QRAMM frameworks; determine appropriate PQC migration phasing; critically evaluate quantum computing ROI timelines and vendor claims.

**Create**
: Design a three-phase enterprise quantum roadmap; construct a cryptographic bill of materials (CBOM); build a quantum center of excellence staffing plan.

## Content Scope — 15 Chapters

| Chapter | Title | Key Topics |
|---------|-------|-----------|
| 1 | Classical Bits vs. Quantum Bits | Landauer's principle, qubits, Bloch sphere, n-qubit state space, quantum parallelism |
| 2 | Superposition, Entanglement, and Bell's Theorem | Hadamard gate, Bell states, CHSH inequality, decoherence, T1/T2 times |
| 3 | Quantum Gates and Circuits | Pauli gates, H, S, T, CNOT, Toffoli, universality, no-cloning theorem |
| 4 | Quantum Algorithms | QFT, Shor's algorithm, Grover's algorithm, BQP complexity, PQC mathematics |
| 5 | Quantum Communication | Teleportation, superdense coding, BB84 QKD |
| 6 | Density Matrices and Noise | Mixed states, decoherence channels, QEC preview, adiabatic quantum computing |

| Chapter | Title | Key Topics |
|---------|-------|-----------|
| 7 | Why Noise-Free Quantum Computing Is Difficult | Error types, noise sources by modality, threshold theorem, overhead problem |

### Part III: Business (Chapters 12–15)

|---------|-------|-----------|
