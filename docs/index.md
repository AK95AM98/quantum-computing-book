# Quantum Computing: From Qubits to Business Value

**A Technical and Strategic Guide**

*Prof. Sharat Batra, University of Minnesota — 2026*

---

Quantum computing has crossed from theoretical promise into enterprise-relevant territory. In December 2024, Google's Willow chip demonstrated the first below-threshold quantum error correction in history — confirming that the laws of physics permit reliable quantum computation. In 2025, IBM delivered its Nighthawk and Loon processors, validating the modular architecture that will lead to fault-tolerant machines by 2029. Meanwhile, nation-states are harvesting today's encrypted data for future quantum decryption — a threat that demands action from every organization *now*, years before a cryptographically relevant quantum computer exists.

This book serves two audiences simultaneously: **technically curious executives and strategists** who need to understand *why* quantum computing matters and *what* to do about it, and **graduate students and engineers** who need rigorous foundations. It is written by a professor, for people who expect to be taken seriously.

## What This Book Covers

<div class="grid cards" markdown>

- **Part I — Foundations** (Chapters 1–6)

    The physics of qubits, entanglement, quantum gates, algorithms (Shor's, Grover's), quantum communication, and the mathematics of noise — built from first principles, with worked examples throughout.

- **Part II — Engineering** (Chapters 7–11)

    Why building quantum computers is so hard, how error correction works, detailed vendor roadmaps (IBM, Google, Microsoft, IonQ, D-Wave, NVIDIA), modular scaling architectures, and an evidence-based milestone framework.

- **Part III — Business** (Chapters 12–15)

    Post-quantum cryptography migration, enterprise use cases, organizational readiness assessment, and a concrete CXO action plan with regulatory deadlines and budget guidance.

</div>

## How to Navigate

- **Executives and strategists:** Read Chapters 1–2 for intuition, then jump to Part III. Return to Part I when you need the physics behind a claim.
- **Graduate students:** Work through Parts I and II sequentially. Part III provides context for why the engineering milestones matter commercially.
- **Use the [MicroSims](sims/index.md)** to develop intuition for quantum gates, Grover's algorithm, and the Mosca calculator — interactive simulations that make the mathematics tangible.
- **Use the [Learning Graph](learning-graph/index.md)** to see concept dependencies and plan your reading path.

## The Central Message

!!! tip "Why 2026 Is Different"
    Previous quantum hype cycles lacked commercial grounding. Three things changed: (1) quantum error correction now works below threshold — confirmed independently by Google and China's Zuchongzhi team; (2) the quantum technology sector crossed $1 billion in annual revenue in 2025; and (3) NIST finalized post-quantum cryptography standards (FIPS 203/204/205) with regulatory deadlines starting 2027. **PQC migration is a compliance imperative that begins now. Quantum computing is a strategic exploration with a credible 5–10 year horizon.**
