# Meet Quarky

**Quarky** is the mascot of this textbook. As a friendly qubit, Quarky lives in quantum superposition and helps explain key concepts throughout the book.

## What Quarky Demonstrates

| Button | What Happens | Concept Illustrated |
|--------|-------------|---------------------|
| **▶ Start** | Quarky begins animating — color shifts between blue and pink | Superposition: α\|0⟩ + β\|1⟩ |
| **Measure** | Quarky collapses to \|0⟩ (blue) or \|1⟩ (pink) randomly | Wave function collapse / measurement |
| **Entangle** | Quarky glows gold and displays \|Φ⁺⟩ | Entanglement and Bell states |
| **Reset** | Returns Quarky to superposition | Reversibility before measurement |

The **Bloch sphere** indicator (top right) shows Quarky's quantum state as a vector — pointing up for \|0⟩, down for \|1⟩, and anywhere on the sphere for a superposition.

## Learning Objectives

After interacting with Quarky, you should be able to:

1. Explain why a qubit differs from a classical bit
2. Describe what *superposition* means visually
3. Understand why *measurement* is irreversible
4. Recognize the Bloch sphere representation of a qubit state
